## Problem
SMO's `Scripter.EnumScript()` generates `CREATE USER` and `CREATE ROLE` but does NOT generate `ALTER ROLE ... ADD MEMBER` statements. Role memberships were silently lost on export.

## Design Decisions
- **Dedicated folder (`01_Security_RoleMembers`):** Mirrors `11_Tables_ForeignKeys` pattern — role memberships are a second pass that depends on roles and users (from `01_Security`) already existing. A separate folder guarantees correct dependency ordering without relying on filename hacks.
- **Raw SQL, not SMO Scripter:** Role memberships aren't SMO-scriptable objects. Used `CustomData.Statements` on work items with a `RoleMembers` special handler that writes SQL directly via `File.WriteAllText`.
- **Fixed roles included:** Users in `db_owner`, `db_datareader`, etc. are exported too — these are critical for permissions even though the fixed roles themselves aren't scripted.
- **`dbo` excluded:** `dbo` is always `db_owner` by default; including it would cause import errors on target databases.
- **Umbrella exclusion:** When `Security` is excluded, role memberships are automatically skipped (function returns early). `RoleMembers` can also be excluded independently.
- **Backwards/forwards compatibility:** Old exports missing `01_Security_RoleMembers` are silently skipped by the importer (folder not in `$orderedDirs`). New exports used by old importers that lack the folder in their `$orderedDirs` also silently skip it.
